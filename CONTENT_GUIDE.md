### Why Did You Pay? Exists

Barrier-free parking is incredibly convenient.

You simply drive in, park, leave when you’re ready and pay later online.

**Because leaving is so effortless, paying later becomes surprisingly easy to forget.**

Once you’re home or busy with the rest of your day, the parking is no longer in front of you. Your attention moves on, and a payment you fully intended to make can simply slip your mind.

The easier it is to leave, the easier it is to forget to pay later.

The challenge isn’t remembering. It’s remembering before the payment deadline.

That happened twice within our family in just a few weeks, resulting in unnecessary penalty charges.

The first happened after collecting our daughter from the airport. Everyone arrived home, unloaded the luggage, put the kettle on, caught up on the holiday and looked through photos. The intention to pay was there, but life moved on and the payment was forgotten until two days later.

Just a few weeks later, the same thing happened again. Our son dropped his fiancée at the airport before heading to work. He also intended to pay later, but only remembered four days afterwards, when another unnecessary penalty charge could no longer be avoided.

Those experiences inspired one simple question:

Did You Pay?

That question became the idea behind an app that helps people remember before a forgotten payment becomes an unnecessary penalty charge.
